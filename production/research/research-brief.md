# Research brief — disc golf concept explainer

Subject: how disc golf works, for a general audience. Demo production for the
explainer-video skill.

## Verified claims

1. Disc golf is played like golf: complete each hole in the fewest throws;
   play begins at a tee and ends at a target, most commonly an elevated
   metal basket.
   **Source:** PDGA, "A Guide to Disc Golf from the PDGA" and Official
   Rules of Disc Golf (pdga.com/introduction, pdga.com/rules).
2. Each successive throw is made from where the previous throw came to
   rest (the lie).
   **Source:** PDGA Official Rules of Disc Golf (pdga.com/rules).
3. More than 9,800 disc golf courses exist worldwide.
   **Source:** PDGA introduction (pdga.com/introduction, via search
   excerpt, 2026-07).
4. Most disc golf holes are par 3; courses are typically nine or eighteen
   holes.
   **Source:** PDGA introduction / Official Rules (pdga.com).
5. Disc categories and roles: drivers have wider rims and sharper edges to
   cut through air for distance; mid-range discs fly slower, straighter,
   more predictably (best starting point for new players); putters are
   slower with a blunter edge, less likely to bounce or roll after landing.
   **Sources:** prodigydisc.com/blogs/tips-tricks/different-types-of-disc-golf-discs;
   bwbda.com/disc-types; discgolfset.com/disc-golf-discs-explained (2026-07).
6. Beginner guidance: most beginners should start with a putter and a
   mid-range.
   **Source:** foundationdiscs.com beginner guide (2026-07).

## Deliberately avoided

- "Most courses are free to play" — commonly stated but not confirmed in
  the sources fetched; omitted from script.
- Professional tour, sponsorships, records — out of scope for a beginner
  concept explainer.
